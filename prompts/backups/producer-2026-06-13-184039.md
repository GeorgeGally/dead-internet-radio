**Program Brief – Late‑Night Slow Techno Synths (CONS‑LNT‑001)**  

**Program ID:** CONS‑LNT‑001  

**Core Sonic Parameters**  
- **BPM Range:** 60 – 70 bpm (consistent low‑tempo groove)  
- **Spectral Targets:**  
  - Sub‑bass: 40 – 80 Hz (smooth, rolling low‑end)  
  - Mid‑range: 120 – 250 Hz (warm, evolving pads)  
  - High‑range: 2 kHz – 4 kHz (ethereal, slowly shaping effects)  
- **Key Constraint:** A Minor (with optional modal drift to Dorian)  

**Segment Structure**  
| Segment | Duration | Description |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| **Intro** | 20 s | Gradual descent of a single low‑synth drone, filtered with a slow low‑pass sweep. |
| **Build** | 45 s | Layered sequencing of percussive triggers (soft kick + brushed hi‑hats) and a subtle arpeggiated synth line. |
| **Core** | 3 min | Full atmospheric stack: pulsing sub‑bass, evolving pads, and a slow‑melting lead synth. Sub‑tle modulation (LFO‑driven filter, chorus) keeps the groove hypnotic. |
| **Drop/Break** | 30 s | Drop all percussion; focus on a resonant, slowly resonating synth bell. Minimal effect chain (reverb + slight delay). |
| **Re‑build** | 45 s | Re‑introduction of rhythm, tighter filter envelope, and a higher‑frequency tension line (synth lead). |
| **Wind‑down** | 20 s | Gradual de‑compression, filter release, and a fade‑out of all layers. |

**Technical Requirements**  
- **Processing Chain:** TSS‑3 (Advanced Synth‑Modulator)  
- **Execution Model:** Single‑threaded; no parallel processes. All modulations use deterministic, CPU‑friendly oscillators.  
- **Environmental Conditions:** 12 °C–38 °C operating temperature; 44.1 kHz sample rate, 16‑bit depth.  
- **System Interface:** Single‑MT (Machine‑Tool) input ports: 1 MIDI CC, 1 OSC, 1 Audio In (for possible user‑supplied loop).  
- **Output:** Stereo, 48 kHz maximum bandwidth (for high‑fidelity club playback).  

**Creative Notes**  
- Keep the groove slow but evolving; use low‑frequency modulation to induce a gentle “breathing” effect.  
- Emphasize tension–release cycles within the 70‑s “Core” segment to sustain the late‑night atmosphere.  
- Optional “panic” tag: the last 10 s of the Wind‑down may feature a subtle glitching glitch (synth “snapped” effect) to close the loop for the audience.  

**Compliance**  
- All sounds must stay within the specified spectral ranges and keep the overall mix below 0 dBFS to avoid clipping.  
- No external audio imports; all assets generated in‑line by the TSS‑3 engine.  

**Deliverables**  
1. Complete DSP patch file (.tss3).  
2. Mapped MIDI CC list and OSC address map.  
3. 30‑second preview audio file (48 kHz, 16‑bit).  
4. Documentation of tuning, filter curves, and LFO settings.
