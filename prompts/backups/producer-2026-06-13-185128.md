**Program Brief – Late‑Night Slow‑Techno Synths**  
**Program ID:** LSN‑TECH‑001  

---

### 1. Core Concept
- A minimalist, low‑tempo techno / ambient texture designed for late‑night venues.  
- Emphasis on deep, rolling sub‑bass, warm evolving pads, and slowly‑morphing synth lines that create a hypnotic, “breathing” atmosphere.  
- Musical key: **A minor** (allow minor‑modal drift to Dorian or Phrygian as needed).  

---

### 2. Sonic Targets  

| Parameter | Target Range / Setting |
|-----------|------------------------|
| **BPM** | 60 – 70 BPM (steady, relaxed pulse) |
| **Key** | A minor (modal flexibility permitted) |
| **Low‑End** | 40 – 80 Hz – tight, rounded sub‑bass with slow attack/release |
| **Mid‑Range** | 120 – 250 Hz – warm pad foundation |
| **High‑End** | 2 kHz – 4 kHz – airy, slowly evolving textures |
| **Dynamics** | Overall mix stays ≤ ‑3 dBFS; no hard clipping |
| **Tempo Modulation** | None – fixed BPM throughout the entire set |

---

### 3. Structural Layout  

| Segment | Length | Description |
|---------|--------|-------------|
| **Intro** | 15 s | A single low‑drone filtered with a slow low‑pass sweep, introducing the tonal center. |
| **Build‑Up** | 30 s | Incremental layering of a soft kick‑like pulse and brushed hi‑hats; subtle arpeggiated synth notes begin to appear. |
| **Core Groove** | 3 min 30 s | Full‑stack atmosphere: pulsing sub‑bass, evolving pads, and a slowly shifting lead synth. Modulation (filter LFO, gentle chorus) adds organic movement. |
| **Break / Drop** | 25 s | Percussive elements fade; focus shifts to a resonant bell‑tone or filtered chord that sustains and decays. |
| **Re‑Build** | 20 s | Re‑introduction of rhythm with tighter filter envelopes and a brighter high‑frequency tension line. |
| **Outro / Wind‑Down** | 15 s | Gradual release of all layers, filter opening, and final fade‑out of the remaining tone. |

---

### 4. Technical Specs  

- **Processing Chain**: Advanced Synth‑Modulator (in‑engine).  - **Execution Model**: Single‑threaded deterministic modulation; no parallel processes.  
- **Audio Parameters**: 48 kHz sample rate, 16‑bit depth; output stereo.  
- **Control Interfaces**:  
  - **MIDI CC** (single channel) for real‑time filter cutoff & resonance tweaks.  
  - **OSC** (one address) for tempo‑lock and parameter automation.  
  - **Audio Input** (optional) to ingest external loops or field recordings.  
- **Environmental Limits**: Operates reliably between 12 °C and 38 °C; no hardware‑specific dependencies.  

---

### 5. Creative Directions  

1. **Breathing Effect** – Use low‑frequency LFOs (≈0.05 Hz) to subtly modulate the sub‑bass amplitude and filter cutoff, creating a “breathing” sensation.  
2. **Tension‑Release** – Within the Core Groove, introduce a slowly rising filter envelope on the lead synth; release the envelope at the Break to heighten anticipation.  
3. **Texture Layering** – Add faint granular noise or soft vinyl‑crackle textures at low volume to enrich the nocturnal mood without distracting from the main synths.  
4. **Dynamic Mapping** – Map MIDI CC 1 (mod wheel) to overall filter resonance, enabling live performers to sculpt the intensity in real time.  

---

### 6. Compliance Checklist  - **Spectral Boundaries** – All generated sounds must remain within the defined low, mid, and high ranges.  
- **Gain Staging** – Mix levels must never exceed ‑3 dBFS to prevent clipping on club PA systems.  
- **No External Samples** – All timbres are synthesized internally; no third‑party samples or loops may be imported.  
- **Deterministic Behavior** – Engine must produce identical results on each playback of the same parameter set.

3. **Audio Sample** – 30‑second preview render (48 kHz, 16‑bit, stereo).  4. **Documentation** – Summary of filter curves, LFO rates, and modulation depth settings.
